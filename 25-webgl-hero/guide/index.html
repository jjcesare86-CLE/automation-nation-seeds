Ingot Atelier

A bespoke goldsmith and jewelry atelier. The WebGL raymarched liquid-metal hero is the concept: the shader IS the molten metal in the crucible. A luxury small-batch jewelry brand where the hero animation is the material.

Concept

The full-viewport hero is a signed-distance-field metal blob evaluated per pixel in a single fragment shader — the visual signature of a workshop where every piece is poured, not machined. Cursor position feeds displacement; idle time (≥3s no cursor movement) increases displacement so the metal appears to "breathe" in its crucible. The typography over the shader is the atelier's voice: quiet, first-person plural, one piece per week.

Why this works for a jewelry brand

  • Bespoke jewelers, custom engagement rings, artisan goldsmiths — this niche demands a "material as identity" hero. The shader shows the raw metal in its most seductive state (molten, moving).
  • Zero photography needed. Perfect for a brand that doesn't want to photograph client pieces once they leave the workshop.
  • Rebrandable to any small metalworks: silversmith, bronze casting studio, custom watchmaker's case shop.

Files that hold this site together

  • brand.json — brand tokens, palette, contact.
  • index.html — copy blocks marked data-editable="hero.headline" etc.
  • styles.css — palette bound to --void, --bone, --mercury, --gold, --dim. All motion easing declared once at the top.
  • app.js — WebGL 1.0. Fragment shader lives inline as a template string. To rebrand the metal color, change the three color vec3s near the bottom of main(): mercury, bone, gold.

Rebrand in 15 minutes

  1. Edit brand.json — atelier name, goldsmith name, city, email, palette.
  2. Update the five CSS custom properties in styles.css :root to match.
  3. Update the three vec3s in app.js — mercury, bone, gold — to the same palette. For a silversmith, keep mercury + bone but neutralize the gold accent to platinum. For a bronze studio, warm the mercury toward copper.
  4. Rewrite text in the data-editable blocks: hero.headline, hero.sub, pieces.lead, pieces.body, atelier.about.
  5. Point the mailto link and footer to the new email + address.
  6. Update the piece-type <select> options in the commission form to match what the atelier actually casts.

Signature techniques

  • Fresnel-Schlick rim on the SDF for a warm bone-over-mercury metal look.
  • FBM displacement driven by u_mouse, u_time, and an u_idle ramp.
  • Vignette + tone-map + gamma for cinematic finish.
  • Reduced motion — respects prefers-reduced-motion: time freezes, one static frame.

Copy voice cheat sheet

  • First-person plural. "We cast one piece a week."
  • Never "handcrafted," "artisan," "one-of-a-kind." Say the specific thing — the crucible, the wax, the invested mould, the finish afternoon.
  • Reference the goldsmith by name. The atelier is a person, not a brand.
  • Weekly commission cap is the scarcity signal — do not remove it.
