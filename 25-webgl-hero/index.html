how a surface remembers its own weight.

A raymarched signed-distance field, animated in fragment. No mesh. No model. One equation, one loop, one long look at the light.

Read the study →
§ 01

The metal is not modelled. There is no vertex here, no mesh, no ambient occlusion pass. Every frame is a fresh sphere-traced field, evaluated per pixel against a distance function that folds itself around a central mass, pulled toward the cursor, quieted by time.

When you leave the page idle, the field breathes. When you return, it remembers. The lighting model is a two-term reduction of the classic Fresnel-Schlick approximation, tuned for a warm bone highlight over a mercury body — the same palette a jeweller would use on a wax carving under a north-facing window.
