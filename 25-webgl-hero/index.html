§ 01

Every piece is cast from raw stock — 18k yellow, 18k rose, sterling, sometimes platinum — melted in a graphite crucible over a torch you can hear from the street. The wax is carved by hand, the mould is invested in the same room, and the piece is finished on the same afternoon it is cast.

Recent commissions include a signet in 18k yellow for a family in Antwerp, a pair of wedding bands with matched inscription in rose, and a small cast pear-shaped pendant intended, we were told, for a christening. We do not photograph pieces once they leave the atelier. What you see below is the crucible — the constant, not the product.

The atelier is run by Marek Wójcik, third-generation goldsmith, formerly a bench for a house in Antwerp. Commissions begin with a 90-minute seated consultation in the workshop, coffee included, no obligation. If the piece is right for us, we quote it in writing within a week and hold your slot in the queue.

§ 02 · Begin a commission

A short letter before you visit.

Tell us who the piece is for, roughly when it needs to be worn, and any material you're already thinking about. We reply in one business day. If the piece is right for us — and we're right for it — we invite you to the workshop for a coffee and a sketch.

Marek Wójcik, Goldsmith
